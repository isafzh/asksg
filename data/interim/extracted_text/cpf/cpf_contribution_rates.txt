Determine the year of SPR status
Here’s how to determine the year of SPR status:
The first year begins on the day of SPR conversion
The second year begins on the first day of the month after the first anniversary of SPR conversion
The third year begins on the first day of the month after the second anniversary of SPR conversion

Step 3: Determine the Total Wages subject to CPF contributions
Apply the wage ceilings to determine Total Wages subject to CPF contributions
An employee’s CPF contributions are computed based on the Total Wages payable in a calendar month.
Total Wages are made up of an employee’s Ordinary Wages and Additional Wages. Different ceilings apply to Ordinary Wages and Additional Wages.

Step 4: Compute the CPF contributions on Total Wages
Compute Total CPF contributions
You can compute the Total CPF contributions by applying the contribution rate to the employee’s Total Wages subject to CPF. Total CPF contributions for the employee comprise of your share and that of your employee’s.
false
Round off your Total CPF contributions
Please round off the Total CPF contributions to the nearest dollar. Cents should be dropped for amounts less than 50 cents. Amounts of 50 cents and above should be treated as an additional dollar.
You’re entitled to recover the employee’s share of CPF contributions when paying your employee’s wages for the month. The cents should always be dropped for the employee’s share of CPF contributions.
