Things to note
What you need to know before making cash top-up or CPF transfer
Top up to boost your monthly payouts
Consider the amount of monthly payouts you wish to have to support your desired retirement lifestyle, and top up to work towards your goal.
Try using the
Retirement Payout Planner
or
Monthly Payout Estimator
to facilitate your financial planning.
false
Top-ups are irreversible and reserved for monthly payouts
Top-ups (both cash top-ups and CPF transfers) are irreversible as they are a long-term commitment to boost your retirement savings for higher payouts.
They cannot be withdrawn for any other purposes
.
false
Consider your financial situation and goals before making the top-up
For example, consider whether you plan to finance your house using your Ordinary Account savings or cash.
false
Find out your top-up limits and tax benefits
Visit your
Retirement Dashboard
to find out your top-up limits (the maximum amount you can top up) and eligible tax relief benefits.
false
Quick tip
Make your cash top-up or CPF transfer early to earn more through the power of compounding interest. If you make a top-up in January instead of December, you can earn up to 20% more interest in just 10 years on those monies.

Cash top-ups
Who you can make a cash top-up to
1
Yourself and your loved ones
Receive up to $8,000 tax relief when you make a cash top-up to yourself and up to another $8,000 when you make a cash top-up to your loved ones (parents, parents-in-law, grandparents, grandparents-in-law, spouse and siblings) in each calendar year.
The top-up
amount you or your loved ones can receive
and
tax relief you can enjoy
depends on the recipient’s age and CPF balances. If your recipient is eligible for the Matched Retirement Savings Scheme, the top-up amount which attracts the matching grant will not enjoy tax relief.
Tax relief only applies to givers and not recipients.
false
2
Your employees or platform workers
Boost your employees' or platform workers’ retirement savings by making cash top-up on your employees' or platform workers’ behalf.
By doing so, your cash top-up will be tax-deductible as a business expense, while your employees or platform workers
may enjoy tax relief
of up to $8,000.

true
Apply online
3
Any other CPF member (e.g. friends or other relatives)
Help grow the savings of people you care about so they can enjoy higher payouts in retirement.
Note that such top-ups are not eligible for tax relief.
false
CPF transfers
Who you can make a CPF transfer to
Besides cash top-ups, you can also transfer your CPF savings to yourself and your loved ones to boost your own or their retirement savings.
Do note that CPF transfers do not qualify for tax relief and the matching grant under the Matched Retirement Savings Scheme (MRSS).
1
Yourself
You can transfer your Ordinary Account (OA) savings to your Special Account (SA) if you are below 55.
If you are aged 55 and above, you can
transfer your OA savings to your Retirement Account (RA).
If you would like to transfer your monthly CPF employment contributions regularly from your OA to RA, consider setting up
recurring CPF transfers
.
The amount you can transfer depends on
your age and CPF balances
.
false
2
Loved ones
Before transferring your CPF savings to your loved ones, you have to first set aside enough for your own retirement.
The amount of CPF transfers you can make to them depends on
how much your loved one can receive
, and the
amount you can transfer
.
false
